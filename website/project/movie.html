Maynila: Sa Kuko ng Liwanag

Lino Brocka

Based on the novel of the 50's, the follows Julio Madiaga, a young man who searches for his lost girlfriend in the corrupt and brutal underworld of Manila. This landmark film explores themes of poverty, exploitation, and social injustice.
A must mandatory watch one of the internationally praised, critically acclaimed coming from the the great Filipino filmmaker

Rating: 5/5

Kisapmata

Mike de Leon

Based on the real life case reported by the great National Artist Nick Joacquin, the film narrates about a controlling father who becomes obsessively fixated on his daughter after intense family conflicts.
Such controversies and changes away from the real events, the film explores themes of control, paranoia, and the destructive effects of authority and repression within a family, which metaphorically reflects the political societies during the dictatorial regimes of the 70's. Coming from as cinematographer of the great Lino Brocka, this landmark film became the relevant , cautionary tale. A must watch.

Rating: 4/5

Buybust

Erik Matti

BuyBust (2018) is an action-thriller about PDEA units (anti-drug agency) trapped in a Manila slum during a botched drug raid, fighting for survival against ruthless criminals.
Such inconsistent camera work, over-the-top action-packed performance, and one of the unrealistically most killed extras, most death tolls, one of the highest body counts in Philippine cinema in just one location, it highlights themes of corruption and the brutal realities of the drug war. A critique of the terrible anti-drug war campaign.

Rating: 3.9/5

Hello, Love, Again

Cathy Garcia Sampana

Hello, Love, Again (2024), directed by Cathy Garcia-Molina, is the sequel to Hello, Love, Goodbye. It follows Joy (Kathryn Bernardo) as she heals from a breakup and finds new love with Ethan (Alden Richards). The film explores themes of healing, second chances, and the complexities of love and forgiveness.
Another big grossing profit films coming from the two biggest rivaled entertainment industries.

Rating: 4/5

Lolo and the Kid

Benedict Micque

Lolo and Kid (2022) is a Filipino drama film that tells the heartwarming story of a young boy and his grandfather, who develop a deep bond despite their generational differences. As they navigate life's challenges together, the film explores themes of family, love, and the importance of intergenerational relationships.

Rating: 4.8/5

Himala

Ishmael Bernal

Himala (1982), directed by Ishmael Bernal, stars Nora Aunor, a young woman who claims to have witnessed a miracle, causing a religious frenzy in her village. As people flock to her, Nora grapples with the pressure of faith, expectation, and exploitation.
The film explores belief, sacrifice, and human nature, and is considered a classic of Philippine cinema.

Rating: 4/5

White Lady

Jeff Tan

White Lady (2006), directed by Jeff Tan, is a Filipino horror film about Mia, a woman who moves into a new house and is haunted by a vengeful White Lady spirit. As the haunting intensifies, Mia uncovers the ghost's tragic past, revealing themes of revenge and guilt. The film blends Filipino folklore with suspense, exploring the emotional consequences of past actions.
As underrated as heck, it was the staple horror film that scares when we were kids.

Rating: 4/5
